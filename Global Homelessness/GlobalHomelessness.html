Global Homelessness

Worldwide

In 2005, the United Nations attempted a global survey in which it was estimated that 100 million people were homeless worldwide, and around 1.6 billion people lacked adequate housing.

Africa

Within Kenya, There are around 250,000 people without homes in Nairobi alone. More than 72% of Zimbabweans live below the poverty line, and 62% of the households have also been deemed poor. It is estimated that there are 24.4 million homeless Nigerians alone. This is a result of the country’s unemployment rate, which was estimated a 11.3% in 2014. However, is believed to be a huge underestimation. Africa’s conditions prevent accurate data from gathering.

Asia and Oceania

In August, 2016, there were 116,427 people experiencing homelessness in Austria. The homeless population has increased by 13.7% since 2011. In Cambodia, there are around 20,000 street victims of human trafficking, and more than 180,000 people live in informal settlements. "Coffin homes", "cage homes", and roof tops seem to be around 100,000 people's reality in Hong Kong. That's 40% of Hong Kong's entire population. Aswell as this, there are over 3 million Japanese people who move from cafe to cafe each night, better known as "Internet cafe refugees". Phillippines' capital city, Manila, is inhabited by over 3.1 million homeless people. This is te largest population of any city in the world. It is estimated that 1.2 million Phillipino children sleep rough, with 70,000 in Manila. In Spain, there are about 40,000 homeless people, with an additional 1.5 million families living in shelters.

North America

It is estimated that more than 235,000 Canadians experience homelessness in a year, with more than 35,000 Canadians homeless on any given night. The prime reason people in this area become homeless is due to hurricanes. In 2004, one of the worst hurricanes in the history damaged 90% of the homes and left approximately 60,000 people (more than half of the population) homeless. There are also over 475,000 people are believed to have absolutely no housing at all. Around 8,000 street children are at risk of recruitment by street gangs. More than 40 million people were living in poverty, and 18.5 million were living in deep poverty, with reported family income below one-half of the poverty threshold. The implications of the great recession are still felt. An estimated 3.2 million jobs were eliminated, and 1.2 million homes foreclosed upon following during the financial crash between 2007-2009. In Mexico City, an estimated 50% of people live in informal, low-income settlements. The 2010 earthquake in Haiti left 2.3 million Haitians homeless, 170,000 of whom are still living in makeshift housing. Even before the earthquake, an estimated 70% of the population of Port-au-Prince lived in slums.

South America

Every night in Argentina, there are 15,000 reported homeless people in Buenos Aires, 4,500 of whom are children. Brazil, with a population of 210 million, has over 50 million Brazilians live in inadequate conditions. Out of the 3.8 million Columbian households, nearly 30% of them lack what is needed to live. In Venezuela 90% of the population live in poverty. Since 2014 more than 3.4 million Venezuelans have left the country.This was one of the largest cases of forced displacement in the western hemisphere. On average, in 2018, 5,000 people left Venezuela every day.
